Case Study 01 · Procore Technologies

Enterprise Agentic AI Platform — 45% to 90%+ Response Accuracy

GenAI / AgenticProduction3,000+ Customers
+

Problem

Procore's AI assistant was returning ~45% accurate responses — hallucinating project data, surfacing wrong documents, and failing on multi-domain queries. Enterprise customers were churning on AI features before they could demonstrate ROI. The platform had no grounding strategy and no evaluation framework to measure or improve.

Strategy & Execution

  • Designed supervisor orchestration layer with intent-routing to domain-specific agents via real-time manifest resolution.
  • Built just-in-time grounding workflows with schema-driven action layers to eliminate hallucinations.
  • Established Evaluation-as-a-Service with transaction-level metrics: correctness, recall, and completeness.
  • Shipped multimodal RAG pipelines supporting text, document, and image intelligence across all enterprise tiers.
  • Defined AI governance: agent registries, versioning, ML exclusions, and hybrid-cloud compliance across AWS, GCP, Azure.

Case Study 02 · Autodesk, Inc.

License-to-Subscription Platform Migration — $1B ARR

Platform / API$1B ARRBusiness Transformation
+

Problem

Autodesk was executing one of the most complex commercial transformations in enterprise software — moving from perpetual licenses to subscriptions. The existing infrastructure had no transactional API layer for subscription creation, entitlement management, or partner order placement at scale. Failure would mean broken revenue recognition and permanent customer trust damage.

Strategy & Execution

  • Led design of transactional subscription APIs — GET/POST microservices for partner order placement, pricing, and product management.
  • Defined full API contracts: schema, entities, filtering/sorting, error handling, rate limits (<250ms latency, 10K req/user).
  • Built order-to-subscription sync: .com → SAP (order creation) → Salesforce (subscription with entitlements and product line items).
  • Delivered AI/ML churn and buying-readiness models contributing to 25% YoY revenue growth.
  • Architected NLP support bot (IBM Watson) resolving ~20K cases/month, saving $1M+ annually.

Case Study 03 · Procore Technologies

360° Executive Reporting — Near-Real-Time at Petabyte Scale

Data PlatformDistributed SystemsCAP Theorem Applied
+

Problem

Enterprise customers ($500M+ ARR accounts) were threatening non-renewal because cross-domain reporting did not exist. Data was fragmented across Financials, PMQS, and Resource Management. Full materialization guaranteed consistency but required 45 minutes — an eternity for executives making real-time project decisions. Customers needed data in under 5 minutes.

Strategy & Execution

  • Designed partition strategy: top 50 enterprise customers received dedicated high-volume partitions; remaining customers shared an optimised partition.
  • Introduced incremental materialisation — trading bounded staleness for 5-minute data availability with full consistency guaranteed within 10 minutes.
  • Audited and migrated DynamoDB → DocumentDB → native MongoDB, achieving <250ms P99 SLO on complex nested aggregations.
  • Built cross-domain reporting models joining Financials, PMQS, and Resource Management into a unified 360° executive view.

Leadership Philosophy

The way I
think about
building

Four principles that guide every product, every architecture decision, every team I lead.

  • Outcomes over output

    I measure success by accuracy, retention, and revenue — not velocity or features shipped. Every decision traces back to a customer problem and a business metric.

  • Technical depth enables trust

    The best product leaders earn engineering trust by understanding system constraints. I can audit a query plan, review an API contract, and challenge an architecture decision.

  • Clarity as competitive advantage

    Complex systems fail at the handoff. I invest in clear specs, explicit tradeoffs, and shared understanding — so teams build the right thing the first time.

  • AI is infrastructure, not magic

    Great AI products need evaluation frameworks, feedback loops, and governance from day one. I've built the pipelines that turned 45% accuracy into 90%+ — the unglamorous work that makes AI reliable.
